About IU‑HIP

The Institute for Unified Higher‑Dimensional Information Physics (IU‑HIP) is an independent research institute dedicated to understanding information, geometry, and physical laws within higher‑dimensional hierarchical structures.

IU‑HIP aims to construct and systematize a unified theoretical framework centered on R‑layer Mode Theory (RLMT), a foundational approach to describing the deep informational architecture underlying the universe.

Mission

Our mission is to explore the fundamental principles that govern higher‑dimensional information structures and to develop a coherent theoretical foundation that unifies physical laws, geometric structures, and informational dynamics.

Research Areas

  • Higher‑dimensional information geometry
  • R‑layer Mode Theory (RLMT)
  • Unified physical law structures
  • Quantum information and spacetime structure
  • Hierarchical cosmology and multi‑layer dynamics

Philosophy

IU‑HIP is founded on the belief that the universe is fundamentally an information‑structured entity, and that understanding its layered architecture is essential for unifying modern physics and advancing our knowledge of reality.
